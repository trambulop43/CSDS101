1. In-Depth: Propositional Logic

1.1 Propositions

A proposition is a declarative statement that is either True or False, but never both. It must be a definitive fact or assertion.

Valid Propositions

  • 1. Manila is in the Philippines. (True)
  • 2. $1 + 1 = 3$. (False, but valid)
  • 3. Today is Monday. (Contextual, but definitive)
  • 4. Java is a programming language.
  • 5. If it rains, the ground is wet.

Not Propositions

  • 1. Close the door! (Command)
  • 2. What time is it? (Question)
  • 3. $x + y = z$ (Unknown variables)
  • 4. Oh my god! (Exclamation)
  • 5. This sentence is false. (Paradox)

1.4 Tautology vs. Contradiction

Tautology

A compound proposition that is ALWAYS TRUE, regardless of the truth values of its variables.

Contradiction

A compound proposition that is ALWAYS FALSE, regardless of the truth values of its variables.
