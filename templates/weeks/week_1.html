1. In-Depth: Propositional Logic

1.1 Propositions

A proposition is a declarative statement that is either true or false, but not both. It is the fundamental unit of logic. We typically use lowercase letters like $p, q, r$ to represent them.

Valid Propositions

  • "Paris is in France." (True)
  • "1 + 1 = 3." (False)
  • "It is raining today." (True/False depending on fact)

Not Propositions

  • "What time is it?" (Question)
  • "Clean your room!" (Command)
  • "x + 1 = 2" (Variable x makes truth value unknown unless defined)
