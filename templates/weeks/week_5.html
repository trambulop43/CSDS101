1. The Core Distinction: Order

In discrete math, the most important question you must ask when selecting items is: "Does the order matter?"

Permutations ($P$)

ORDER MATTERS

Arranging items in a specific sequence. Changing the position creates a new outcome.

  • Example: A PIN Code (1-2-3 is different from 3-2-1).
  • Keywords: Arrange, Schedule, Rank, Order, Line up.
  • Formula: $P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$

Combinations ($C$)

ORDER DOES NOT MATTER

Selecting a group of items. Changing the position does NOT create a new outcome.

  • Example: Fruit Salad (Apple & Banana is same as Banana & Apple).
  • Keywords: Choose, Select, Group, Committee, Team.
  • Formula: $C(n,r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$

2. In-Depth: The Formulas Explained

Why $\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$?

Imagine you have 5 racers ($n=5$) and you want to award Gold, Silver, and Bronze ($r=3$).

5 x 4 x 3

Gold x Silver x Bronze

Mathematically, this is taking $5!$ but stopping after 3 spots. Dividing by $(5-3)! = 2!$ effectively "cancels out" the remaining runners (4th and 5th place) who didn't win.

Why divide by $r!$?

Suppose you picked {Alice, Bob, Charlie}. In a Permutation, ABC, ACB, BAC, BCA, CAB, CBA are 6 different wins.

But in a Combination (a Team), these are all the SAME team.

We counted too many!

Since order doesn't matter, we divide the permutation result by $r!$ (the number of ways to arrange the chosen winners) to remove the duplicates.

$C(n,r) = \frac{P(n,r)}{r!}$
