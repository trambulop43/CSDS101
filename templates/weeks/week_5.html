1. Permutations vs Combinations

Permutation ($P$)

Order MATTERS

Arranging items in a specific sequence (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd place).

$_nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$

Combination ($C$)

Order DOES NOT Matter

Selecting a group of items (e.g., a committee of 3 people).

$_nC_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}$

2. Example Problems

Q: License plate with 3 letters?

Use Permutation (or product rule) because ABC is different from CBA.

Q: Fruit salad with 3 fruits?

Use Combination because {Apple, Banana, Orange} is the same salad as {Orange, Apple, Banana}.
