1. Core Theory: Sets & Logic

1.1 The Real Number System

Real Number Hierarchy

[Image of venn diagram set theory]

Definitions

  • Natural Numbers ($\mathbb{N}$): Counting numbers starting at 1. $\{1, 2, 3...\}$
  • Whole Numbers ($\mathbb{W}$): Natural numbers plus Zero. $\{0, 1, 2...\}$
  • Integers ($\mathbb{Z}$): Whole numbers and their negative counterparts. $\{..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2...\}$
  • Rational Numbers ($\mathbb{Q}$): Any number that can be written as a fraction $p/q$. Their decimals either terminate (0.5) or repeat (0.333...).
  • Irrational Numbers ($\mathbb{I}$): Numbers that cannot be written as a simple fraction. Their decimal expansions never end and never repeat (e.g., $\pi$).

1.3 Logical Equivalence Laws

These laws act like algebraic rules for simplifying logic and sets.

1. De Morgan's Laws

"Distributing a negative flips the sign."

Logic Form:

$\neg(p \land q) \equiv \neg p \lor \neg q$

The negation of (p AND q) is (NOT p OR NOT q).

Set Theory Form:

$(A \cap B)' = A' \cup B'$

The complement of an intersection is the union of complements.

2. Idempotent Laws

"Repeating the same thing doesn't add value."

Logic Form:

$p \lor p \equiv p$

Set Theory Form:

$A \cup A = A$
